Who We Are

A nonprofit built on the belief that reentry is a community responsibility.

Reborn Ready Corp is a Macon-based nonprofit organization dedicated to reducing recidivism through a comprehensive, integrated approach to reentry. We don't offer isolated services — we build systems of support that address housing, employment, and wellbeing together.

As the mission holder and grant applicant for the Reborn Ready ecosystem, we oversee program design, fund development, community partnerships, and outcome tracking. Our work is grounded in Macon-Bibb County and designed to meet people where they are — on the day they come home.

We serve justice-involved adults at moderate-to-high recidivism risk, with specialized tracks for veterans and individuals in recovery from substance use disorders.

What We Do

Programs built around the whole person.

📋

Case Management

Individualized support plans connecting clients to housing, employment, benefits, counseling, and community resources — coordinated by trained case managers.

🏛️

Reentry Navigation

Guidance through the practical challenges of reentry: ID restoration, benefits enrollment, court obligations, transportation, and more.

🔧

Workforce Development

Earn-while-you-learn skilled trades training in partnership with GaChi Handyman Services. Clients build a résumé, earn income, and develop real-world skills simultaneously.

🎖️

Veterans Reentry Track

A dedicated program for justice-involved veterans offering housing priority, VA benefits navigation, peer support, and connection to veteran-specific employment opportunities.

🤝

Community Partnerships

We work alongside Macon-Bibb County agencies, faith organizations, employers, and courts to create a coordinated reentry safety net for returning residents.

📈

Recovery Support Track

Coming Year 2: a dedicated track for individuals in recovery from substance use disorders, combining housing stability with peer recovery coaching and treatment linkage.

Population Served

Who we're here for.

Primary Population

Justice-involved adults with moderate-to-high recidivism risk and no stable housing plan upon release from incarceration.

Veterans Track

Justice-involved veterans navigating reentry alongside service-connected challenges, including PTSD, disability, and VA system complexity.

Recovery Track (Year 2)

Adults in recovery from substance use disorders who need housing stability as the foundation for sustained sobriety and community reintegration.

Geographic Focus

Macon-Bibb County, Georgia. We prioritize deep local impact over broad geographic reach — knowing our community is how we serve it well.
